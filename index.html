Emergency Rental & Financial Assistance Program

Providing critical financial relief to prevent homelessness and support community stability

Overview

Our Emergency Rental & Financial Assistance Program is designed to support individuals and families facing temporary financial crises that could result in eviction, homelessness, or utility shutoff. By offering timely financial help and supportive services, we aim to stabilize households, prevent displacement, and promote long-term self-sufficiency.

Program Objectives

  • Prevent evictions and reduce homelessness by covering overdue rent and utility bills.
  • Provide emergency support to families affected by job loss, health emergencies, or unforeseen events.
  • Connect clients to long-term support resources such as job placement, financial education, and housing counseling.

Who Can Apply?

This program is available to residents who are:

  • Facing imminent risk of eviction or homelessness
  • Experiencing a sudden loss of income due to job loss, illness, or other emergencies
  • Renters with a legal lease agreement or homeowners with utility bills in their name
  • Household income below 80% of the Area Median Income (AMI)

Eligible Assistance

We may provide financial aid for the following:

  • Past-due rent payments
  • Upcoming rent if future instability is documented
  • Utility bills (electricity, gas, water)
  • Security deposit and initial rent for new housing (in extreme cases)

Required Documentation

To apply, you must upload clear copies or scans of the following:

  • Government-issued photo ID (e.g., driver's license, passport)
  • Proof of current address (lease agreement, utility bill, or similar)
  • Proof of income or income loss (pay stubs, termination notice, unemployment letter)
  • Eviction notice or disconnection notice (if applicable)
